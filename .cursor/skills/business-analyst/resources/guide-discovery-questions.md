# Product Discovery Questions

Use these questions as a thinking partner when exploring new features or system changes.

## 1. The "Who" (Stakeholders)
- Who is the primary beneficiary of this change?
- Who is negatively impacted (even indirectly)?
- Who needs to approve this?

## 2. The "What" (Capability)
- What problem are we actually solving? (The "5 Whys")
- What is the user doing *right now* to solve this? (The baseline)
- What does the "perfect" solution look like in an ideal world?

## 3. The "Why" (Value)
- Why build this *now* instead of something else?
- What is the cost of NOT building this?
- How will we measure if this was successful?

## 4. The "How" (Interaction Design)
- How does the user enter this workflow?
- What is the most common "Happy Path"?
- What are the three most likely "Error Paths"?

## 5. The "When" (Constraint/Context)
- In what physical or digital environment is this being used?
- How often is this task performed? (Hourly, weekly, annually?)
- Is there a deadline or external pressure driving the timing?

## 6. The "So What?" (Synthesis)
- If we only built 20% of this, which 20% provides 80% of the value?
- What is the one thing that, if it fails, makes the rest of the feature useless?
